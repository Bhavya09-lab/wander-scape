Local Customs

1. Blend of Gujarati and Portuguese Traditions Cultural Harmony: Locals practice both Hindu and Christian traditions with equal respect. Language: Gujarati is widely spoken, but many older residents also speak Portuguese or understand it due to colonial influence.

2. Fishing & Sea Worship Traditions Fisherfolk Communities: Fishing is a major livelihood; communities like the Kharwas have their own sea-based customs. Sea Worship:Nariyal Poornima (Coconut Festival) involves offering coconuts to the sea for safe voyages. Boats are often decorated and blessed before the fishing season.

3. Traditional Dance and Music Garba and Dandiya Raas: Popular during Navratri, danced in local attire with devotion. Portuguese Fado Music: Occasionally sung or played at heritage homes or during Diu Carnival.
