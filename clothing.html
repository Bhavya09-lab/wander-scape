Traditional Attire of Gujarat – Colors of Culture

Explore the vibrant and diverse clothing traditions of Gujarat, where every thread tells a cultural story.

Traditional & Modern Styles

Chaniya Choli

Colorful traditional attire worn by women during Navratri and festivals, typically paired with dupatta.

Fabric: Cotton, Silk, Mirror Work
Festival: Navratri, Weddings
Fun Fact: Each region in Gujarat has its own embroidery pattern for Chaniya Choli.

Patan Na Patola

Intricately handwoven double ikat silk sarees made in Patan, renowned globally for their precision and heritage.

Fabric: Pure Silk
Festival: Weddings, Traditional Ceremonies
Fun Fact: A single saree can take up to 6 months to weave due to its complex design.

Kediyu & Dhoti

Traditional men's outfit consisting of flared top (Kediyu) and pleated lower (Dhoti), often worn during Garba.

Fabric: Cotton, Khadi
Festival: Navratri, Janmashtami
Fun Fact: Kediyu’s flared design enhances movements during folk dances.

Bandhani Saree

Famous tie-dye patterned saree with dots and vibrant colors, symbolic of Gujarat’s artistic textile culture.

Fabric: Cotton, Georgette, Silk
Festival: Raksha Bandhan, Diwali
Fun Fact: The word "Bandhani" comes from the Sanskrit word "Bandh" meaning to tie.

Gharchola

Traditional wedding saree, typically red with golden checks and motifs, worn by brides in Gujarat.

Fabric: Silk with Zari work
Festival: Weddings, Religious Rituals
Fun Fact: The checks symbolize blessings and prosperity in married life.

Angrakha

Regal garment worn by men in royal courts, often paired with churidar and turban, now a fashion statement in festive wear.

Fabric: Cotton, Silk, Brocade
Festival: Weddings, Cultural Ceremonies
Fun Fact: Originally worn by Rajput warriors for both battle and celebration.
