Major attractions include:

Kankai Mata Temple

A sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kankai, located deep inside the Gir forest. It’s an important spiritual site for locals and a peaceful escape for nature lovers.

Google Map

Devaliya Safari Park

A fenced area within Gir for a quick and safe wildlife experience. It's ideal for those with limited time. You can easily spot lions, deer, leopards, and peacocks here without a full forest safari.

Google Map

Sasan Gir Village

The central village that acts as the entry point for Gir safaris. You’ll find many resorts, local craft shops, and guides here. It’s a great place to explore the local culture and cuisine of the Maldhari and Siddhi communities.

Google Map

Khodiyar Maa Temple, Galdhara Dhari

Located in Dhari town near Gir, this serene hilltop temple is dedicated to Khodiyar Mata, a revered deity of Gujarat. The temple is known for its panoramic views, natural springs, and peaceful environment that attracts devotees and trekkers alike.

Culture & Traditions of Gir

Spiritual Essence

1. Sacred Coexistence with Nature: Gir Forest is not just a wildlife sanctuary; it is a sacred landscape where nature and spirituality blend. Locals believe that the forest is a divine creation and treat its wildlife, especially lions, with reverence and respect, as manifestations of divine strength. Maldhari tribes, who live inside the forest, practice eco-spirituality—living in harmony with the environment and treating nature as a guardian spirit.

2. Mythological and Religious Importance Kankai Mata Temple: Located deep inside Gir Forest, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Kankeshwari (Kankai Mata), the protector of the forest and its people. Devotees and even forest officers consider her blessings crucial for safety. Banej Mahadev Temple: A secluded Shiva temple, believed to be a place where Lord Krishna worshipped Lord Shiva. It attracts pilgrims who believe it is a spot of spiritual energy and divine vibrations.

Festivals

Kankai Mata Utsav: Where: Kankai Mata Temple (deep inside Gir Forest) When: During Navratri (September–October) and also on local full moon days What:Devotees from nearby villages visit the temple on foot Aarti, bhajan-kirtan, and folk rituals are performed Local Maldharis offer coconuts and sweets to the goddess Why: Kankai Mata is considered the protector of Gir Forest and its people

Local Customs

1.Respect and Coexistence with Lions: Custom: Locals treat the Asiatic lions not as threats, but as divine beings and part of their ecosystem. Belief: Spotting a lion is considered auspicious, and harming wildlife is taboo. Practice: Maldharis often say, “Sher hamara rakshak hai” (The lion is our protector).

2. Maldhari Tribal Lifestyle Who: Semi-nomadic herders living inside the forest Customs:Live in traditional nes (mud huts) inside the forest Follow oral traditions of storytelling, songs, and spiritual chants Deeply eco-spiritual, treating forests, rivers, and animals as sacred
