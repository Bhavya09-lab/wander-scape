Navratri

Navratri is Gujarat‚Äôs most iconic celebration, marked by nine nights of Garba and Dandiya Raas in honor of Goddess Durga. Cities come alive with vibrant traditional attire, music, and devotion.

Uttarayan (Makar Sankranti)

Celebrated on January 14th, Uttarayan fills the skies with colorful kites. It's a joyful harvest festival where people gather on rooftops, fly kites, and enjoy sweets like chikki and laddoos.

Diwali-The Festival of Lights

Diwali is celebrated with great enthusiasm across Gujarat. Homes are lit with diyas and decorated with rangoli as families worship Goddess Lakshmi and celebrate the triumph of light over darkness.

Rath Yatra-The Chariot Festival of Jagannath

Held in Ahmedabad,Bhavnagar Rath Yatra involves grand processions of Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra through city streets. It draws thousands of devotees every year.

Tarnetar Fair-A Traditional Marriage Fair

Tarnetar Fair is a cultural festival rooted in tribal matchmaking traditions. It features folk dances, music, traditional costumes, and local handicrafts, attracting visitors from across India.
