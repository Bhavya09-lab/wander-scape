Folk & Traditional Arts of Gujarat

Celebrate the creativity of Gujarat through its intricate art forms passed down generations.

Art Forms

Rogan Art

A rare cloth painting technique using castor oil-based colors, practiced in Nirona village.

Materials: Castor oil, natural pigments
Region: Nirona (Kutch)
Fun Fact: Practiced by just one family in India for over 300 years.

Mata Ni Pachedi

Hand-painted or block-printed textiles used as temple hangings and sacred cloths.

Materials: Cotton, natural dyes
Region: Ahmedabad
Fun Fact: Called the "Kalamkari of Gujarat."

Pithora Painting

Ritualistic wall paintings by the Rathwa community, depicting gods and ancestral spirits.

Materials: Lime plaster walls, natural pigments
Region: Chhota Udaipur
Fun Fact: Traditionally painted only after a priest’s command.

Ajrakh Printing

Traditional block printing using resist-dyeing techniques and natural colors on cotton fabric.

Materials: Cotton, Indigo, Madder
Region: Ajrakhpur (Kutch)
Fun Fact: Process involves 14-16 stages and takes several days.

Bandhani Art

Ancient tie-dye art creating intricate dot patterns and vibrant color blends on fabric.

Materials: Cotton, Silk
Region: Jamnagar, Rajkot
Fun Fact: The technique dates back to the Indus Valley Civilization.
