Sacred Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Krishna. Built over 2,500 years ago and reconstructed in the 16th century. It features 72 carved pillars and a towering shikhara.

Nageshwara jyotirlingas

The Nageshwar Jyotirlinga Temple, located about 12 km from Dwarka, is one of the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva. Nestled on the coast of Saurashtra in Gujarat, this powerful spiritual site is believed to protect devotees from all forms of poison and evil forces. The temple houses a striking 25-meter tall statue of Lord Shiva, which can be seen from a distance and adds to the divine ambiance of the place. According to the Shiv Purana, the Nageshwar Jyotirlinga is the first among the twelve Jyotirlingas, where Lord Shiva appeared to protect his devotee Supriya from a demon.

Culture & Traditions of Dwarka

Spiritual Essence

Dwarka is one of the four Char Dham pilgrimage sites and is deeply rooted in Krishna devotion. The Dwarkadhish Temple is not just a religious site but a cultural hub with daily rituals, aartis, and festivals.

Festivals

Local Customs

Many pilgrims take a holy dip in the Gomti River before visiting the temple. Traditional Gujarati attire and Krishna bhajans are a common sight.
