Flavours of Gujarat – A Citywise Culinary Journey

Gujarat's cuisine is a perfect blend of taste and tradition. Each city offers a unique twist to classic dishes, from the spicy and tangy street food of Surat to the royal thalis of Rajkot. Let's explore the culinary treasures city by city.

City-wise Specialties

Ahmedabad

Khakhra, Fafda-Jalebi, Dhokla, Khandvi - perfect breakfast choices often enjoyed during festivals like Navratri.

Surat

Locho, Ghari, Bhusu, and Undhiyu make Surat the food capital of Gujarat—snacking here never stops, even at midnight!

Vadodara

Sev Usal, Bhakharwadi, and Ratalu Puri fill the streets with fragrance and flavor, especially at night stalls.

Rajkot

Kathiyawadi Thali, Bajra Rotla, Garlic Chutney offer fiery flavors from Saurashtra’s heartland.

Bhavnagar

Gathiya, Chavanu, and Khaar are popular tea-time snacks, often served during social gatherings.

Junagadh

Doodh Pak, Malpua, and chilled Chaas are festive delights often seen during religious ceremonies.

Jamnagar

Dry Kachori and Mukhwas make perfect travel snacks—long-lasting and full of flavor.

Gujarati Thali

A traditional Gujarati thali is a symphony of flavors. Dhokla, Thepla, Kadhi, Dal, Khichdi, Pickles, and Chaas bring together sweet, spicy, and tangy in perfect balance.
