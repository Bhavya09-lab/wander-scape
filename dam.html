"Kamleshwar Dam – The Crocodile Hub of Gir"

"Located deep inside Gir National Park, Kamleshwar Dam is a scenic reservoir built over the Hiran River. It plays a vital role in the region’s water supply and supports the surrounding ecosystem. But what makes it truly special for visitors is the thrill of spotting marsh crocodiles basking on its banks."

Major attractions include:

Tulsishyam Temple & Hot Springs (~41 km)

A revered shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu (Shyam) with healing hot water springs, nestled inside the Gir forest. A peaceful mix of spirituality and nature.

View on Google Maps

Girnar Hills – A Sacred Climb to the Skies

Ancient rock hills with over 9,000 steps, sacred to Hindus and Jains. Offers divine views, holy temples, and a rewarding spiritual trek.

View on Google Maps

Anti‑Gravity (Hill) Road

A bizarre road illusion near Tulsishyam where cars roll uphill. An exciting optical trick and a must-experience stop for curious minds.

View on Google Maps

Sana Caves

Rock-cut Buddhist caves near Vankiya village. Peaceful, historical, and ideal for explorers interested in ancient Indian architecture.
